littleLEARNERS





The Problem

The necessary and abrupt transition to online learning has created new challenges for elementary students and their families.

Scheduling

Elementary aged children, k-5, cannot follow a schedule unaided.

Navigation

Finding the location of the virtual course content is unreasonably complicated for elementary aged children due to a lack of digital literacy and an inadequate reading comprehension level.

Busy Lives

Because of the demands of daily life, it is difficult for parents to consistently facilitate the virtual learning process for their children.

Performance

Students who otherwise would perform at the same level may exhibit diminished performance compared to their peers depending on the quality of assistance available in the home.

Learning Platforms

The numerous online learning platforms currently in use for instruction create confusion for parents and caregivers while trying to locate assignments and instructional links.

Communication

K-5 level students do not have the digital literacy, reading comprehension, writing, and typing skills needed to initiate communication with, or receive communication from, their teachers directly.

The Solution

A child-friendly desktop application that functions independently from existing learning platforms in order to provide schedule updates, navigation aid, and direct communication access with the teacher.

  • Schedule Prompting and Updates

    Schedule information and real-time updates are maintained by the instructor and presented to the student via a friendly desktop application.

  • Simplified Navigation

    Links provided alongside schedule prompts ensure children navigate to the correct location at the correct time for synchronous or asynchronous activities.

  • Reach out for Help

    Students are able to contact the teacher directly when assistance is needed outside of live instruction through a video/voice messaging system.

  • Stay Connected

    A corresponding mobile application allows parents to monitor interactions between the teacher and student, as well as to view schedules and updates. This also gives parents a platform to provide feedback.

Current Process Flow

The existing process flow of the elementary virtual classroom

During the mandated 6 hour virtual classroom day, there is only about 2 hours of synchronous learning where the student has direct access to their instructor. During asynchronous learning and assigned activities, which comprise most of the instructional day, getting direct assistance from the teacher is impossible without assistance by a parent or caregiver. It is also neccesary to adhere to a set schedule and navigate between numerous learning platforms in order to complete assignments and attend P.E., art, and music.

Comparison Matrix

Key features of the Little Learners desktop application compared across a number of competitors.

Key features of the Little Learners application include real-time schedule updates, a virtual assistant to help aid a child with navigating between numerous learning platforms and provide schedule prompts, and a K-5 friendly interface. While other applications include similar features such as a corresponding mobile application for parental oversight and feedback options, others are tailored to the Little Learners target demographic, such as the messaging system.

Chat features included in Seesaw, Schoology, and Google Classroom can be difficult to locate for young students and, in most cases, require the ability to read and write at a level beyond that of an average K-5 student in order to effectively communicate and use the service. The Little Learners video messaging option gives students direct access to their instructor during periods of asynchronous learning presented in a format that does not require literacy to operate. Little Learners is a desktop application that operates independently of the established learning platforms, and so, unlike its competitors, can be accessed freely at any time without the need to navigate to specific web applications.
